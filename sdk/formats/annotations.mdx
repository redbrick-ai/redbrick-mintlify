class="pre">[source]</span></span></a><a
href="#redbrick.types.task.Point2D" class="headerlink"
title="Link to this definition">#</a></dt>
<dd>
<p>2D pixel point.</p>
<p>The Point2D coordinates are normalized in relation to the dimensions
of the image, meaning they are scaled down to a range between 0 and 1.
To convert these normalized points back to their original scale on the
image, you “un-normalize” them by multiplying by the image’s dimensions.
This operation typically results in a floating-point number.</p>
<p>RedBrick AI enhances the precision of annotations on its canvas by
<strong>supporting a resolution higher than that of the underlying
image</strong>. This capability facilitates sub-pixel annotation
accuracy. However, if sub-pixel precision is not necessary for your
application, you can simplify the process <strong>by rounding the
un-normalized coordinates to the nearest whole number</strong>. This
adjustment converts the precise floating-point values back to standard
pixel coordinates, making them easier to work with for general
purposes.</p>
<div class="admonition important">
<p>Important</p>
<p>The origin point (0,0) from which these coordinates are measured is
at the top left of the image. However, for mammography and DBT the
origin is the top right of the image.</p>
</div>
